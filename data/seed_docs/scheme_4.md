# Scheme 4 — Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana)

## Overview
Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY is a government-funded health assurance scheme that provides **cashless** coverage of up to **₹5 lakh per family per year** for **secondary and tertiary** care hospitalization at **empanelled public and private hospitals** across India (portability enabled).

## Eligibility
- State:
  - Implemented by States/UTs through State Health Agencies; coverage and enrollment workflows can vary by State/UT.
- Age:
  - **No cap on age** of family members (and no cap on family size).
- Income:
  - Not based on an “income slab” form for everyone. Beneficiary families were initially identified from **SECC 2011** (deprivation criteria for rural + occupational criteria for urban). States/UTs may also use other databases of similar socio-economic profile for verification/expansion.
- Category:
  - Primary identification: **SECC 2011**-based entitled families (bottom ~40% as per scheme design).
  - Also includes families earlier covered under **RSBY** even if not present in SECC 2011.
  - Recent expansions (policy updates): additional groups have been included over time (e.g., certain frontline workers and senior citizens 70+ as per government updates).
- Occupation:
  - Urban eligibility is linked to **occupational criteria** (as per SECC-based identification).

## Checklist
(Enrollment/verification is database-driven; exact documents can vary by State/UT and the enrollment channel.)
- Aadhaar (used as the **primary identity document** for beneficiary identification/verification where applicable)
- Mobile number (OTP/contact)
- Family identification / SECC details as available in the PM-JAY beneficiary database (CSC/hospital operator searches and verifies)
- Any additional proof requested by the enrollment point (CSC/empanelled hospital) based on State/UT workflow
