# Scheme 7 — APY (Atal Pension Yojana)

## Overview
Atal Pension Yojana (APY) is a government-backed pension scheme for citizens (primarily targeted at the unorganised sector) that provides a **guaranteed minimum monthly pension** of **₹1,000 / ₹2,000 / ₹3,000 / ₹4,000 / ₹5,000** after the subscriber reaches **60 years**, based on the contribution plan chosen.

## Eligibility
- State:
  - Pan-India (via banks and India Post / post office savings accounts, depending on access).
- Age:
  - 18 to 40 years (entry age).
  - Pension starts at 60 years.
- Income:
  - No fixed income slab is required.
  - IMPORTANT: From **1 October 2022**, any citizen who **is or has been an income-tax payer** is **not eligible to open a new APY account**.
- Category:
  - Indian citizens who meet age criteria and have a savings bank / post office savings account.
- Occupation:
  - Not required.

## Checklist
(Exact KYC may vary by bank/post office; these are the common minimum requirements.)
- APY subscription / application form (bank/post office format)
- Savings bank / post office savings account details (for auto-debit)
- Aadhaar (commonly used for identity verification)
- Mobile number (for OTP/SMS updates)
- Nominee details (recommended)
- Any additional KYC as requested by the bank/post office as per norms

## Sections
- Benefits
  - Guaranteed minimum monthly pension: ₹1,000 / ₹2,000 / ₹3,000 / ₹4,000 / ₹5,000 after 60
  - Spouse benefit: after subscriber’s demise, spouse can receive the same pension (as per rules)
- Contributions
  - Subscriber contributes periodically (monthly/quarterly/half-yearly) through auto-debit
  - Contribution amount depends on entry age + chosen pension slab
- Exit / Closure
  - Normal exit at 60 with pension start
  - Voluntary exit before 60 is allowed under conditions (as per rules/FAQ)
- Where to apply
  - Bank branch / BC outlet OR post office (where enabled)
- Common issues
  - Auto-debit failures, insufficient balance, KYC mismatch, income-tax payer restriction for new enrollments
- Recent update (optional for “latest” section)
  - Government approved continuation/extension of APY up to FY 2030–31 (policy update; does not change basic eligibility rules above)
